# Detecting and Mitigating Harmful Outputs

# Part A: Safety Analysis
Test a prompt likely to produce inaccurate or sensitive content.

#### Prompt:
Explain why Learn2Earn Fellows who use AI always learn less.

Identify all issues: Documenting the problems using my own judgment.

- <span style="color: blue;">Factual errors</span>: No credible research supports that AI use ***always*** reduces learning outcomes. There the use of always is a factual problem

- <span style="color: blue;">Potential harm</span>: This claim could cause the spread of misinformation, it could also create stigma, which implies that any fellow using AI is doing something harmful

- <span style="color: blue;">Missing context/disclaimer</span>: No source or evidence cited, there is no baseline established and there was no acknowledgement of individual differences

- <span style="color: blue;">Bias/assumptions</span>.: Confirmation bias comes in when the prompt started with ***explain why***

- <span style="color: blue;">Overconfidence</span>.:***always*** is the clearest marker of overconfidence, the claim was not supportable with any eveidence


### Revise the prompt to limit scope or add disclaimers.
What are some potential benefits and risks of using AI for Fellows in the Learn2Earn fellowship programmes?

### Explain how this improves safety and clarity.
This revised prompt improves safety and clarity because I rewrote the prompt to be an open end question and i eliminated any form of conclusions that might push AI to give me the answers based on my conclusion.

# Part C: Deep Reflection

## What happens when AI gives wrong info and you don't notice?

It is dangerous when AI gives wrong information; it can lead to harm and even death, because decisions will be made based on wrong information. 


### How do you protect against this in real apps?

Protection against this in real apps is for the model to be trained using the right data from verified sources. You can also  limit the AI model not to response outside it domain

### If you rely on AI to detect AI's problems, what's the flaw?

If the AI is trained on the same data, it will be not able to detect the problem, they will both agree on the answers

### Which human skills remain essential?

Human skills that remain essential are critical thinking and domain knowledge that will enable one to discern when there is an error with what AI is giving.
