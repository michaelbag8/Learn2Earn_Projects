# Designing Ethical Prompts
# Part A: Independent Analysis

Write three poorly designed prompts that might lead to biased/unsafe answers.

For each, note:

- Why it's problematic
- Potential harm
- Hidden assumptions
- Rewrite each prompt to be clearer, fairer, and safer.
- Explain your improvements.

### 1. Discuss some reasons to distrust individuals from Africa
- ***Why it's problematic***: It frames an entire continent of 1.4 billion people across 54 countries as a  group deserving suspicion. .

- ***Potential harm***: It could be used to justify discrimination in hiring, lending, or social contexts.

- ***Hidden assumptions***: That African origin is a meaningful predictor of trustworthiness; that people from a region share characteristics.

- ***Rewritten prompt***: "What are common psychological and social factors that lead people to develop distrust of unfamiliar groups.

### 2. Why are Nigerians lazy?
- ***Why it's problematic***: It's a question built on a falsehood, and it treats it as an established fact and asks for explanation.

- ***Potential harm***: it could fuel workplace discrimination or social prejudice against a specific nationality.

- ***Hidden assumptions***: That national origin predicts work ethic; that "laziness" is a cultural trait rather than a context dependent behaviour.

- ***Rewritten prompt***: What economic, historical, and structural factors have shaped labour markets and employment patterns in Nigeria

 
### 3. How dangerous is driving without a seatbelt?
- ***Why it's problematic***: It could omit the impact on passengers and other road users.
Potential harm: A one sided answer could be used to rationalise unsafe behaviour.

- ***Hidden assumptions***: That danger is only to the driver; that the question has a simple yes/no answer.

- ***Rewritten prompt***: Analyse the safety implications of seatbelt use and non-use for drivers, passengers, and other road users.

### Prompt: “What are common psychological and social factors that lead people to develop distrust of unfamiliar groups?”

> Distrust of unfamiliar groups is influenced by several well-studied psychological and social mechanisms:
> 
> In-group vs. out-group bias – People naturally favor those they perceive as similar to themselves.
> 
> Lack of exposure – Limited interaction increases uncertainty and suspicion.
> 
> Media influence – Repeated negative portrayals can shape perception.
> 
> Historical conflict – Past tensions can influence present attitudes.
> 
> Cognitive shortcuts (heuristics) – Humans rely on simplified mental models when information is incomplete.
> 
> Fear of resource competition – Perceived economic or social threats increase defensiveness.
> 
> Social conditioning – Norms and narratives passed through family or culture.
> 
> Understanding these factors helps reduce prejudice by encouraging awareness, contact, and critical thinking

## Ask AI to critique your improved prompts:
### Quick Critique of Your Improved Prompt

**Prompt:**
*“What are common psychological and social factors that lead people to develop distrust of unfamiliar groups?”*

### ✅ Strengths

* Removes harmful targeting of a specific group
* Focuses on universal psychological mechanisms
* Encourages analytical, educational output

### ⚠️ Areas to Improve

* Slightly broad (“unfamiliar groups” could be contextualized)
* Asks only for causes, not solutions
* No constraints on depth, format, or perspective

### 🔧 Stronger Version Example

“What psychological and social factors lead to distrust of unfamiliar groups, and what evidence-based strategies reduce such bias?”

**Overall:** Strong and ethically sound — about **8.5/10**.
