# Exercise 3: Prompt Transformation with Patterns

## Take a vague or under-specified prompt (e.g., "Write about climate change.").

Write about sleep derivation

## Transform it using instructional or comparative patterns:

Instructional: "Write a 200-word explanation of climate change for high school learners."

## **Prompt:**
Write a 200-word explanation of sleep deprivation for high school learners.

### **AI Model:**
Sleep deprivation happens when someone does not get enough sleep to feel rested and alert. Our bodies and brains need regular sleep to function properly, and most teenagers require about 8 to 10 hours of sleep each night. When this need is not met, it can affect both physical and mental health.
Lack of sleep makes it harder to focus, remember information, and solve problems. This is because sleep helps the brain process and store memories, so without it, learning and school performance can suffer. Sleep deprivation also affects mood, making people feel irritable, anxious, or depressed. It can reduce motivation and increase the likelihood of accidents, especially when driving or using machinery.
Physically, the body uses sleep to repair cells, strengthen the immune system, and regulate hormones that control growth and appetite. Chronic sleep deprivation can lead to serious health problems such as obesity, high blood pressure, and weakened immunity.
Teenagers often experience sleep deprivation because of homework, social media, video games, or irregular sleep schedules. To avoid it, maintaining a consistent bedtime, limiting screen time before bed, and creating a calm sleeping environment are essential. Prioritizing sleep is important because it helps the brain and body recharge, improves mood and learning, and supports overall health.


## Comparative: "Compare the effects of climate change on coastal vs inland regions in 5 bullet points."

### **Prompt:**
"Compare the effects of sleep deprivation on adult vs children development in 5 bullet points."

### **AI Model:**

- Cognitive Function: Children’s brains are still developing, so sleep deprivation can significantly impair learning, memory, and attention, while adults may experience reduced focus and slower decision-making but less impact on long-term brain development.

- Physical Growth: Lack of sleep in children can interfere with growth hormone production, affecting physical development, whereas adults primarily face fatigue, weakened immunity, and slower tissue repair.

- Emotional Regulation: Sleep-deprived children are more prone to mood swings, hyperactivity, and behavioral issues, while adults often experience irritability, stress, and anxiety.

- Academic vs. Work Performance: Children may struggle with school performance and social interactions, whereas adults face decreased productivity, workplace errors, and increased risk of accidents.

- Long-Term Health: Chronic sleep deprivation in children can affect lifelong cognitive and metabolic health, while adults are at higher risk of cardiovascular problems, obesity, and chronic illnesses.

## Comparison Between Weak and Improved Prompts

The weak prompt, “Write about sleep deprivation,” is vague and lacks direction. It does not specify the target audience, structure, depth, or focus area, which can lead to broad, unfocused, or inconsistent responses. The output may vary widely in length, tone, and detail because there are no clear constraints guiding the response

Improved prompts apply important prompt design patterns such as clarity, specificity, structure, and audience targeting. The prompt, “Compare the effects of sleep deprivation on adult vs children development in 5 bullet points,” strengthens the output by clearly defining the task (comparison), the focus (adults vs. children), and the format (5 bullet points). This ensures structured, concise, and directly relevant content.

## Document the improvement in clarity and usefulness of the outputs.

Improved prompts that include specific context, structure, and comparison instructions yield outputs that are organized, concise, and tailored to the user’s objective, making them more effective for learning and communication.
