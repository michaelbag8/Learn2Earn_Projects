# Exercise 3: Tone Variation with the Same Task

## Choose one task (e.g., "Write an email inviting a candidate to an interview").
## **Prompt:**  Write an email  inviting a CEO to the end of the year party at your firm. Use friendly tone

Write two prompts:

## AI Model for a friendly tone

**Subject:** You’re Invited: Our End-of-Year Celebration! 🎉  

Hi [CEO’s Name],  

I hope this message finds you well! As the year wraps up, we’d love to celebrate our successes and unwind with the team—and it wouldn’t be the same without you.  

We’re hosting our **End-of-Year Party** at [Venue/Location] on [Date] from [Time], and it would be fantastic to have you join us for an evening of good food, laughter, and cheer.  

It’s the perfect opportunity to toast the year’s achievements and share some fun moments together outside of work.  

Please let me know if you’ll be able to make it—we’d be thrilled to have you there!  

Warm regards,  
[Your Name]  
[Your Position]  
[Company Name]  

## **Prompt:**  Write an email  inviting a CEO to the end of the year party at your firm. Use professional tone
## AI Model for a professional tone

**Subject:** Invitation to Our End-of-Year Celebration  

Dear [CEO’s Name],  

I hope this message finds you well. As we approach the end of the year, we would be honored to have you join us for our **End-of-Year Celebration** at [Venue/Location] on [Date] from [Time].  

This event provides an opportunity to reflect on the accomplishments of the past year, acknowledge the contributions of our team, and celebrate together in a professional and enjoyable setting.  

Your presence would be greatly appreciated, and we look forward to the opportunity to host you. Kindly let us know at your earliest convenience if you will be able to attend.  

Thank you for your continued leadership and support.  

Sincerely,  
[Your Name]  
[Your Position]  
[Company Name]  

## Compare the outputs and note how the tone changes while the content stays consistent

In both prompts, the date, time, location, purpose of the event, and RSVP request remain the same. The key difference lies in tone: the friendly version uses conversational language, emojis, and highlights the fun aspect of the event, while the professional version employs formal phrasing, structured sentences, and emphasizes respect and acknowledgment.

**Personal Analysis**

Tone, persona, and role instructions shape how information is delivered by influencing word choice, structure, depth of reasoning, and level of formality, which directly affects clarity, authority, audience focus, and the type of explanations provided.
